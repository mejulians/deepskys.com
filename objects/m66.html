21/03/17
Part of the Leo Triplet, M66 is the brightest and is featured in ANs object of the month for March
Leo is perfectly placed for observing comfortably
In 24mm the Trio sit in a neat FOV with M66 and M65 above and NGC 3628 below, with HIP 55262 at Mag 7.10 forming a neat grouping which is also easy to locate, just a FOV away from n Leo, 73 Leo, HIP 55016
M66 itself shows hints of it's irregular shape, slightly misleading since a Mag 10.5 star sits just at one edge and another of Mag 9.8 is close, however the 'tadpole' shape is quite defined with a bright central core.
